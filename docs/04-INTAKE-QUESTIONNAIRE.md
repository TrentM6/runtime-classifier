# Intake Questionnaire: 7 Questions

The 7 questions used to anchor a classifier in the creator's own intent — *before* portfolio analysis.

The wording below is photography-flavored. For other domains, see `templates/intake/` for pre-adapted versions (art, graphic design, writing, music). Adaptation is mostly word substitution (image → song, frame → page, etc.) — the question structure carries across domains.

---

## How to use

Send the 7 questions to the creator. Ask for **2–3 sentences per answer** — concise beats essay. Don't lead. Resist the urge to interpret as you go.

After all 7 answers come back, write a one-paragraph summary in the creator's own words. That summary is your reference document for Phase 2 (portfolio analysis) and Phase 3 (rule synthesis).

---

## Q1. Why do you make images? What's your visual philosophy?

**Purpose**: Capture **intent** and **belief system**. Not "I like photography" but "why do I make *this kind* of work?"

**Listen for**:
- What draws them to subjects (people, places, concepts, abstractions)
- What they value (truth, beauty, transformation, provocation, comfort)
- Who the work is for (themselves, the world, a specific community)

**Maps to**: rule selection (which dimensions matter at all), and the visual-language preamble.

---

## Q2. Describe your creative process. How do you find moments?

**Purpose**: Understand **how** they work. Reactive vs. constructed. Where they go, what they look for, how decisions get made in real-time.

**Listen for**:
- Location strategy (scout, wander, embed, studio)
- Reactivity (preconceived frames vs. responding to what shows up)
- Decision triggers (what makes them press the shutter)
- Workflow (rapid-fire, deliberate, reshooting)

**Maps to**: rules around documentary/candid quality, decisiveness, context.

---

## Q3. What do you look for in a frame? What makes you click?

**Purpose**: Surface **visual priorities** — the specific compositional and emotional elements that trigger action.

**Listen for**:
- Composition (framing, depth, geometry, subject placement)
- Subject types (people, expressions, situations)
- Emotional cues (what feeling, what kind of moment)
- Technical (light quality, color, contrast, texture)

**Maps to**: composition, subject, and lighting rules — usually 3–4 of the 8.

---

## Q4. What technical decisions matter most to your work?

**Purpose**: Understand **specific technical choices** that define the aesthetic.

**Listen for**:
- Medium (film stock, digital profile, lens character, brushes, instruments)
- Exposure / dynamic range
- Color / white balance philosophy
- Rendering (skin tones, highlights, shadows; texture; detail)
- Sharpness / DoF / focus

**Maps to**: technical rules — film/lens/color/lighting character. Often the most differentiating rules.

---

## Q5. What emotional intent or feeling do you want viewers to experience?

**Purpose**: Define the **emotional target**. Not what the work shows, but how it should *feel*.

**Listen for**:
- Resonance (recognition, feeling, thought)
- Mood (joyful, contemplative, urgent, intimate, playful)
- Distance (celebratory or critical, warm or detached)

**Maps to**: emotional/connection rules.

---

## Q6. What are your constraints or non-negotiables?

**Purpose**: Identify **hard boundaries** — things the creator will never (or always) do.

**Listen for**:
- Subject constraints (will/won't shoot certain things)
- Technical constraints (will/won't use certain techniques)
- Ethical constraints (subject treatment, permissions, dignity)
- Aesthetic constraints (palette, composition, tone)

**Maps to**: rule weights (non-negotiables get higher weight) and explicit "Avoid" lists in GENERATION blocks.

---

## Q7. What consistency patterns do you notice in your best work?

**Purpose**: Meta-reflection. What does the creator *notice* about their own output? Often surfaces patterns they're not fully conscious of.

**Listen for**:
- Recurring subjects, moments, expressions
- Emotional beats that dominate
- Technical patterns (focal length, framing, timing)
- Context (time of day, locations, conditions)

**Maps to**: this is the question that most directly seeds rule candidates. Patterns the creator names *and* the portfolio confirms = strong rules.

---

## After the answers

Cross-check internally:

- Does Q1 (philosophy) align with Q2 (process)? If not, the creator may be aspirationally describing intent that their actual process doesn't reflect.
- Do Q3 (priorities) and Q4 (technique) match? Stated priorities should show up in technical choices.
- Do Q5 (emotional target) and Q7 (observed patterns) align? If "I want viewers to feel intimacy" but Q7 mentions "wide architectural shots," there's a tension worth investigating.

Internal consistency means you have a real, coherent viewpoint to encode. Inconsistency isn't disqualifying — it's information about where the creator is still working things out.
