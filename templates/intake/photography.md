# Photography — Intake Questionnaire

Answer in 2–3 sentences per question. Don't worry about being clever; concise and honest beats polished.

These answers anchor the analysis of your portfolio. They should reflect what you actually do, not what you think sounds impressive.

---

## Q1. Why do you make photographs? What's your visual philosophy?
*(Listen for: subject pull, what you value, who the work is for.)*

> 

## Q2. Describe your creative process. How do you find moments?
*(Listen for: location strategy, reactivity, decision triggers, workflow.)*

> 

## Q3. What do you look for in a frame? What makes you click?
*(Listen for: composition priorities, subject types, emotional cues, technical priorities.)*

> 

## Q4. What technical decisions matter most to your work?
*(Film stock or digital profile, lens character, exposure, color grading philosophy, sharpness/DoF, rendering of skin tones and shadows.)*

> 

## Q5. What emotional intent or feeling do you want viewers to experience?
*(Listen for: resonance, mood, warmth/distance.)*

> 

## Q6. What are your constraints or non-negotiables?
*(Listen for: subject/technical/ethical/aesthetic things you'll never — or always — do.)*

> 

## Q7. What consistency patterns do you notice in your best work?
*(Listen for: recurring subjects, emotional beats, technical patterns, contexts.)*

> 

---

## After you answer

- Read back through. Is there internal consistency? (Q1 ↔ Q2; Q3 ↔ Q4; Q5 ↔ Q7.)
- What's missing? If you can't answer Q4 specifically, your technique may be intuitive — that's fine, just note it.
- Keep this file. It's your reference document for portfolio analysis (Phase 2) and rule synthesis (Phase 3).
