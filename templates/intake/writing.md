# Writing — Intake Questionnaire

Answer in 2–3 sentences per question. Don't worry about being clever; concise and honest beats polished.

These answers anchor the analysis of your portfolio (essays, fiction, journalism, blog posts, scripts, technical writing — whatever your form is). They should reflect what your writing *is*, not what you wish it were.

---

## Q1. Why do you write? What's your writing philosophy?
*(Listen for: what you're after — clarity, beauty, persuasion, exploration, witness. Who are you writing for?)*

> 

## Q2. Describe your process. How does a piece get from blank page to draft?
*(Listen for: planned vs. discovered, outlining, pace, daily routine, where you start — voice, structure, scene, line?)*

> 

## Q3. What do you look for at the sentence level? What makes a line work for you?
*(Listen for: rhythm, diction, imagery, syntax, restraint vs. ornament, what makes you keep a line vs. cut it.)*

> 

## Q4. What craft decisions matter most?
*(POV/voice, tense, paragraph length norms, dialogue handling, scene vs. summary balance, level of abstraction, use of jargon.)*

> 

## Q5. What experience do you want a reader to have?
*(Listen for: visceral vs. cerebral, comfort vs. confrontation, intimacy vs. distance, demanding vs. accessible.)*

> 

## Q6. What are your constraints or non-negotiables?
*(Subjects you avoid, structural choices you refuse, tonal moves that feel false, ethical stances on representation.)*

> 

## Q7. What consistency patterns do you notice in your best work?
*(Sentence length tendencies, recurring images/figures, structural moves, opening/closing patterns, tonal register.)*

> 

---

## After you answer

- For writing: pull 5–10 of your best paragraphs (not whole pieces — paragraphs) and re-read with these answers in mind. Do they confirm what you said?
- The hardest rule for writers to articulate is usually voice. If Q3 felt vague, that's normal — Phase 2 portfolio analysis will surface it.
- Keep this file. It's your reference for portfolio analysis and rule synthesis.
