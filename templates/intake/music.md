# Music — Intake Questionnaire

Answer in 2–3 sentences per question. Don't worry about being clever; concise and honest beats polished.

These answers anchor the analysis of your portfolio (your tracks, recordings, demos, live takes, productions — whatever your role is: composer, performer, producer, engineer). They should reflect what you actually make, not your influences or aspirations.

---

## Q1. Why do you make music? What's your musical philosophy?
*(Listen for: emotional vs. intellectual, performance vs. composition, audience vs. self, tradition vs. experimentation.)*

> 

## Q2. Describe your creative process. How does a track or piece get started?
*(Listen for: instrument-led vs. concept-led, improvisation, demo workflow, when you commit to an idea, where you start — beat, chord, lyric, sound design?)*

> 

## Q3. What do you listen for when shaping a piece? What makes you keep a take?
*(Listen for: groove, harmonic interest, melodic shape, energy, vocal quality, mix character, what's the "yes" moment.)*

> 

## Q4. What technical/production decisions matter most?
*(Instrumentation, recording approach, mic choices, tempo handling, quantization vs. natural feel, mix philosophy, master loudness, analog vs. digital chain.)*

> 

## Q5. What emotional response do you want from a listener?
*(Listen for: visceral vs. contemplative, intimate vs. epic, comfort vs. friction, present-moment vs. nostalgic.)*

> 

## Q6. What are your constraints or non-negotiables?
*(Sounds you'll never use, production techniques you reject, structural choices you refuse, tonal/genre boundaries.)*

> 

## Q7. What consistency patterns do you notice in your best work?
*(Recurring tempos, key signatures, instrumentation, arrangement moves, lyrical themes, mix character.)*

> 

---

## After you answer

- For music: pull 3–5 tracks you consider your best work and listen with these answers in mind. Do the answers describe those tracks accurately, or do you hear gaps?
- Production-heavy work will skew rules toward technical/timbre dimensions; performance-heavy work will skew toward decisiveness, dynamic range, and live-feel rules.
- Keep this file. It's your reference for portfolio analysis and rule synthesis.
