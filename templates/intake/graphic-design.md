# Graphic Design — Intake Questionnaire

Answer in 2–3 sentences per question. Don't worry about being clever; concise and honest beats polished.

These answers anchor the analysis of your portfolio (identity systems, marketing collateral, editorial layout, web/UI, packaging, etc.). They should reflect what you actually ship, not what's in your dream-client folder.

---

## Q1. Why do you design? What's your design philosophy?
*(Listen for: communicate vs. decorate, function vs. expression, what good design *does* in your view.)*

> 

## Q2. Describe your design process. How do you go from brief to first comp?
*(Listen for: research, references, ideation, iteration speed, where you start — type, layout, image, system?)*

> 

## Q3. What do you prioritize in a layout? What makes you commit to a direction?
*(Listen for: grid/structure, hierarchy, type-image balance, white space, reading order, the "first idea" vs. "third idea" rule.)*

> 

## Q4. What technical/craft decisions matter most?
*(Type pairing, color systems, grid conventions, micro-typography, alignment discipline, file/print/web technical standards.)*

> 

## Q5. What response do you want from a viewer encountering your work?
*(Comprehension first? Emotional pull first? Friction-free vs. attention-grabbing? Tonal register: corporate, playful, editorial, austere?)*

> 

## Q6. What are your constraints or non-negotiables?
*(Type families you'll never use, layout patterns you avoid, color choices you reject, ethical limits on clients/messages.)*

> 

## Q7. What consistency patterns do you notice in your best work?
*(Recurring grid choices, palette ranges, type pairings, scale relationships, overall tonal register.)*

> 

---

## After you answer

- For design specifically: separate **systems** (type/color/grid) from **specific moves** (a particular layout idea). Rules will mostly emerge from systems.
- Note where your taste contradicts industry consensus — those are often your strongest signature rules.
- Keep this file. It's your reference for portfolio analysis (Phase 2) and rule synthesis (Phase 3).

See `docs/04-INTAKE-QUESTIONNAIRE.md` and `docs/09-EXTENDING-TO-DOMAINS.md` for how design-specific rules typically map from the photography example. For design, "Message Primacy" is often the highest-weight rule.
