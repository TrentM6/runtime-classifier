# Art / Painting / Drawing — Intake Questionnaire

Answer in 2–3 sentences per question. Don't worry about being clever; concise and honest beats polished.

These answers anchor the analysis of your portfolio (paintings, drawings, sculpture, mixed-media work). They should reflect what you actually do, not what you think sounds impressive.

---

## Q1. Why do you make art? What's your artistic philosophy?
*(Listen for: subject pull, what you value, who the work is for, beliefs about art's role.)*

> 

## Q2. Describe your creative process. How do you start a piece?
*(Listen for: studio practice, source material, sketching/preparation, planned vs. discovered, daily routine.)*

> 

## Q3. What do you look for when making compositional decisions?
*(Listen for: figure/ground priorities, depth, balance, focal points, what makes you commit a mark vs. revise.)*

> 

## Q4. What materials, tools, and techniques matter most?
*(Medium, brushes/tools, surfaces, mark-making approach, layering, edges, finish, scale.)*

> 

## Q5. What emotional or intellectual response do you want viewers to have?
*(Listen for: visceral vs. cerebral, comfort vs. confrontation, recognition vs. mystery.)*

> 

## Q6. What are your constraints or non-negotiables?
*(Subject matter you'll never do, technical things you avoid, ethical stances, aesthetic limits.)*

> 

## Q7. What consistency patterns do you notice in your best work?
*(Recurring subjects, motifs, palette tendencies, mark qualities, scales, surfaces.)*

> 

---

## After you answer

- Read back through. Is there internal consistency? Where do philosophy (Q1) and process (Q2) align or diverge?
- For art specifically: are you describing intent or aspiration? Both are useful, but separate them — the gap is informative.
- Keep this file. It's your reference for portfolio analysis (Phase 2) and rule synthesis (Phase 3).
